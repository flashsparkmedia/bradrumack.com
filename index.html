Client Builds

"DMD (DMD Systems) provides ITAD solutions adhering to the most rigorous environmental and security standards to deliver confidence for companies who value our world and their reputation. DMD works with hundreds of companies and public entities to securely destroy their data, decommission assets, and redeploy or remarket the equipment across IT categories including Data Center, End User Compute, and Mobility."

DMD had recently launched a new product, but soon realized that their shipping processes currently in place didn't scale to fit this new product's needs. They came to me seeking a way to integrate round-trip shipping into ShipStation's API, and then allow end-users the ability to track their shipment both to them and then back to DMD. DMD's clients can also generate company-wide containing all users' shipments. Data from their sales system is imported into the application via CSV, then shipping labels for both directions are automatically generated and and provided via PDF for easy printing.

This application was built on MERN+Redux stack with the following APIs: Google oAuth2, ShipStation, OpenCage, Outlook via nodemailer, UPS, and FedEx via delivery-tracker

Hawkins.png is a photography editing business based in Los Angeles, California. Shawn came to me with a need for a way to streamline his process of onboarding new clients and providing editing services. At the time, everything was handled manually - catalogs were uploaded to Shawn's drive account, and project management was just simply email correspondence between Shawn and his clients and editors.

For Shawn, I created a project management and file-sharing application. Clients are taken through the project creation process where they pick package options and then upload their catalog as a zip file. Projects are then assigned to an editor and follow a lifecycle from editing to review to approval and delivery. Email notifications are automatically sent to applicable parties at certain lifecycle waypoints. A newer feature has new project notifications being sent to Slack, where editors can claim unassigned projects through interaction with a bot. This application was built on MERN stack with the following APIs: Google Drive, Google Workspace email via nodemailer, and Slack.
